# Kova — Brand Context

## What Kova Is (and Is Not)
Kova is **competition infrastructure** — the tool between an athlete's video submission and their official score.

It is NOT:
- A fitness tracker
- A rep-counting algorithm
- A wellness app
- A workout recorder

It IS:
- Officiating software
- A scoring audit trail
- A judging platform with transparency and accountability at its core

## Name & Lineage
**Kova** — from the Russian *kovat'*, meaning to forge metal under heat and pressure into a permanent shape. Kettlebell sport was codified in the Soviet Union. Russian-rooted language signals legitimacy and lineage to serious athletes. Kova reads like modern SaaS to outsiders but carries the DNA of the sport underneath.

## The Problem
Kettlebell sport online competitions have no standardized judging infrastructure:
- Organizers manage judges via WhatsApp
- Judges watch videos on phones with no tooling
- Rep counts go on paper or spreadsheets
- No audit trail. No transparency. No standard.

Kova fixes this with timestamped rep taps, blind judging, and a traceable disputed-rep resolution system.

## Tone & Voice
**Competition infrastructure. Not motivation.**

The UI should feel like a scoreboard or timing system — something that *officiates*, not something that *motivates*.

- Precise. Minimal. Authoritative.
- No gamification language
- No wellness/lifestyle language
- Numbers are the hero — large, legible, dominant
- Sparse copy. Every word earns its place.

## Visual Identity
**Design language: metal colors → black**

Core concept: accent color gradient fading to black. Inspired by how iron kettlebells use color to identify weight — but the bell itself is always black. The color is functional, not decorative.

Two color directions:
- **Silver / Steel** — cold, precise, premium. The shimmer of a brand new competition kettlebell.
- **Deep Bronze / Amber** — warm, earned, legacy. The patina of a well-used bell.

Rules:
- Single accent color at top/behind hero, bleeding into deep black
- No two-color gradients. No rainbow. Just: [metal color] → black
- Dark backgrounds throughout — near-black base
- Typography: large bold numerics, exceptionally legible at size (tabular/monospaced digits)
- Minimal UI chrome — data is the hero
- Accent used sparingly, only for emphasis

## Target User (Primary)
Kettlebell sport coaches and athletes who compete in online competitions. They are serious, technically literate in the sport, and skeptical of tools that feel too casual. They respect rigor.

Secondary: Competition organizers who are currently managing everything manually and are looking for infrastructure they can trust.

## North Star Question
**Would a coach trust this score over a handwritten count?**
