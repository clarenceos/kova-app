# Quick Task 260327-21e: Summary

**Task:** Incremental IndexedDB chunk writes during recording + block iOS recording
**Status:** Complete
**Commit:** cbf188b

## What Changed

**app/record/page.tsx** — Added explicit iOS block in browser support check. iOS passes `captureStream` feature detection on 18.4+ but kills pages under memory pressure during long recordings.

**lib/recording-store.ts** — Added 3 new functions:
- `appendChunk(chunk)` — appends a single Blob chunk to IndexedDB 'chunks' key
- `assembleChunks(mimeType)` — reads all chunks and assembles into a single Blob
- `clearChunks()` — deletes the 'chunks' key

**app/record/recording/page.tsx** — Two changes:
- `MediaRecorder.start(10_000)` — emits data every 10 seconds instead of all-at-end
- `ondataavailable` now calls `appendChunk(e.data)` — each 10s chunk persisted immediately
- `onstop` calls `clearChunks()` after saving final blob

**app/record/playback/page.tsx** — Recovery fallback chain:
1. Try `restoreRecording()` (full blob from onstop)
2. Try `assembleChunks(mimeType)` (incremental chunks from crash)
3. Wait 1s for slow context, then redirect
- `clearChunks()` added to upload complete and discard handlers

## Why

A 10-minute recording on iOS was lost because the OS killed the page under memory pressure during the single large blob write in `recorder.onstop`. With incremental writes, a crash at minute 8 preserves 8 minutes of data. Blocking iOS entirely prevents the scenario from recurring.
